Rural Broadband

Back to Project Examples

Stratsoft supported over 100 broadband applications for government BTOP stimulus funding with a 68% success rate — well above the national average for these highly competitive grants.

For one large BTOP project Stratsoft created the partnership, built the business and network deployment plans and continues to support the project by developing new lines of telecom services business that give the project commercial viability.

The BTOP Challenge

Broadband Technology Opportunities Program (BTOP) grants were among the most competitive federal stimulus awards available — requiring applicants to demonstrate not only unmet need, but a credible business plan, a viable network design, and a clear path to long-term sustainability without ongoing federal subsidy.

Stratsoft's Role

Stratsoft provided end-to-end support across the application lifecycle, including:

  • Identifying under-served communities and quantifying unmet broadband need
  • Designing technically credible fiber network architectures
  • Building financial models demonstrating path to commercial viability
  • Developing partnership structures between anchor institutions, ISPs, and local government
  • Preparing submission documentation meeting all federal requirements
  • Ongoing support to make awarded projects commercially self-sustaining

Notable Outcome — Cape Cod & Southern MA

One landmark BTOP engagement resulted in a successful $24 million award to build a 270-mile fiber network bringing broadband to a historically under-served coastal region. Stratsoft created the partnership, designed the network, and built the financial forecast showing how serving anchor institutions could be combined with commercial demand to create a self-sustaining business.
