Maps

Stratsoft specializes in building and analyzing geo-spatial databases. The resultant data are often depicted as maps — an efficient and effective way for decision-makers to absorb and use large data-sets to decide and plan the future. These data can be viewed as multi-layered files and embedded into a secure web portal that includes data download and geo-analysis functions.

Northern VA — Network Design

This single map shows many different market perspectives — scrolling through reveals:

  • Top Inset: Demand — Bandwidth usage by businesses and clustering with respect to fiber networks
  • Center Inset: Readily Addressable Demand — Buildings economically addressable by existing fiber
  • Bottom Inset: Competition — Location and routes of different fiber networks in the region
  • Main Map: Network Design — Fiber extension and microwave links reaching cost-justified demand

Network design to bring Ethernet Business Services (EBS) to buildings in Northern Virginia. This composite map is available to the client with each data layer togglable, supported by a linked economic model for exploring different scenarios. The result is a network extension business plan with quantified cost/benefit analyses.

Connecting to Cloud Computing — Toronto

This project allowed our client to evaluate how to provide networks to serve Cloud Computing demand. It identified and quantified specific locations of high IaaS demand in Toronto. Minimum spanning networks were then designed to serve each business, as well as a network to serve the majority of locations (dotted red line) with an Optical Virtual Private Network (OVPN).

The analysis enables the client to prioritize network build-out based on the density and value of addressable cloud computing demand.

Broadband Fiber — Cape Cod & Southern MA

This network design was developed as part of a successful submission for $24 million of Government Stimulus (BTOP) funding to build a 270-mile fiber network bringing broadband to an under-served region.

With an associated Financial Forecast, Stratsoft was able to show how serving under-served community institutions with broadband could be combined with serving commercial demand to create a self-sustaining new business.

Serving Businesses Near High-Bandwidth Buildings

Stratsoft often provides data, maps and analysis for its clients as a secure portal on the web. This project identifies the demand for high capacity communications close to high bandwidth buildings and Data Centers.

By overlaying the network 'supply' it can quickly be seen where network extensions can address the strongest demand. Stratsoft often embeds analytical algorithms within geo-spatial software to allow clients to view and download data and undertake analysis as required.
